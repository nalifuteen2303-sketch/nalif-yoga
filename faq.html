What everyone asks first

If you have a question that isn't answered here, drop us a note at hello@kriya.example — we read every message.

Absolutely. Most people who walk through our doors are complete beginners, and our Foundations of Yoga course is built for exactly that. Our teachers will meet you where you are, and every pose has modifications. There is no such thing as "not flexible enough" or "not ready" — the practice meets you.

Just bring yourself. We provide mats, props, and water. Wear comfortable, breathable clothing that lets you move — anything you'd wear to a light workout. Avoid eating a heavy meal in the hour before class.

If you're brand new, start with Hatha or Foundations of Yoga — both are slow, well-paced, and welcoming. If you want a more athletic, breath-led flow, try Astanga. For precise alignment and therapeutic benefits, Iyengar is wonderful. And for energy work and breath, Kundalini. Our front desk is happy to help you pick a starting point.

Yes — private 1-on-1 sessions are available with any of our teachers. They're a great way to deepen your practice, work on specific goals, or recover from injury. Book via the contact page or speak with us at the studio.

We offer monthly, 6-month, and yearly plans for kids, adults, and couples. All include unlimited group classes plus perks like workshops, retreats, and private session credits. See the pricing section on the home page or contact us for details.

Yes. Our retreats are intentionally welcoming — the days are paced, the food is nourishing, and the practice is optional in the gentlest sense. Whether it's your first retreat or your tenth, you'll be met exactly where you are.

Group classes can be cancelled up to 6 hours before start time with no charge. Private sessions require 24 hours' notice. Memberships are cancellable with 30 days' notice. Retreat deposits are non-refundable but transferable to a future retreat.

Yes. Our main studio is step-free with accessible restrooms, and we have chairs, blocks, and bolsters available in every class. If you have a specific accessibility need, please let us know in advance and we'll prepare the space for you.

Yes — our 200-hour teacher training runs twice a year. It's a 12-week immersion covering asana, philosophy, anatomy, sequencing, and practicum. See the courses page for the next start date.

Subscribe to our newsletter on the home page for class updates, retreat news, and quiet letters from the studio. You can also follow us on Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn.
